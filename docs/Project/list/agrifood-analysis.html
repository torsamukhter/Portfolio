Overview

This project was completed as part of BAA1089: Statistical Models and Methods for Business Analytics at Dublin City University, as a group assignment. The goal was to analyse AgriFood Global’s financial performance across 5 regions and 10 product categories from 2015 to 2024, and provide strategic recommendations to senior management.

Note

Dataset: 500 rows × 19 columns, covering weekly transactions from 2015–2024. Variables include Region, Category, Units Sold, Revenue, Cost, Profit, Net Profit, Discount, and Shipping Cost.

Data Loading & Overview

import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
import plotly.express as px
import plotly.graph_objects as go
from plotly.subplots import make_subplots
import warnings
warnings.filterwarnings("ignore")

df = pd.read_excel("../../data/powerbi_data_group1_AgriFoods_Global.xlsx")
df["Year"] = pd.to_datetime(df["Date"]).dt.year

print(f"Dataset shape: {df.shape}")
print(f"\nRegions: {sorted(df['Region'].unique())}")
print(f"Categories: {sorted(df['Category'].unique())}")
print(f"Years: {df['Year'].min()} – {df['Year'].max()}")
print(f"\nTotal Revenue: €{df['Total_Revenue'].sum()/1e6:.2f}M")
print(f"Total Net Profit: €{df['Net_Profit'].sum()/1e6:.2f}M")
Dataset shape: (500, 20)

Regions: ['Africa', 'Asia', 'Europe', 'North America', 'South America']
Categories: ['Accessories', 'Agriculture', 'Automotive', 'Construction', 'Education', 'Electronics', 'Energy', 'Finance', 'Healthcare', 'Travel']
Years: 2015 – 2024

Total Revenue: €136.10M
Total Net Profit: €8.33M

Overall Financial Performance (2015–2024)

Code 
yearly = df.groupby("Year")[["Total_Revenue","Total_Cost","Total_Profit","Net_Profit"]].sum().reset_index()

fig = go.Figure()
colors = {"Total_Revenue": "#4C72B0", "Total_Cost": "#DD8452",
          "Total_Profit": "#2ecc71", "Net_Profit": "#e74c3c"}
labels = {"Total_Revenue": "Total Revenue", "Total_Cost": "Total Cost",
          "Total_Profit": "Total Profit", "Net_Profit": "Net Profit"}

for col in ["Total_Revenue", "Total_Cost", "Total_Profit", "Net_Profit"]:
    fig.add_trace(go.Scatter(
        x=yearly["Year"], y=yearly[col],
        name=labels[col],
        mode="lines+markers",
        line=dict(color=colors[col], width=2),
        marker=dict(size=6),
    ))

fig.update_layout(
    title="AgriFood Global: Financial Performance 2015–2024",
    xaxis_title="Year",
    yaxis_title="Amount (€)",
    template="plotly_white",
    height=400,
    hovermode="x unified",
)
fig.show()

Total Revenue, Cost, Profit and Net Profit trends from 2015–2024

WarningKey Finding

Revenue has not increased since 2019 — a concerning trend for the company. Despite declining revenue, net profit has shown some improvement, suggesting cost-cutting rather than growth is driving margins.

Regional Analysis

Revenue and Profit by Region (2015–2024)

Code 
region = df.groupby("Region")[["Total_Revenue", "Net_Profit", "Total_Profit", "Shipping_Cost"]].sum().reset_index()
region["Gross_Margin_%"] = (region["Total_Profit"] / region["Total_Revenue"] * 100).round(2)
region["Net_Margin_%"] = (region["Net_Profit"] / region["Total_Revenue"] * 100).round(2)

fig = make_subplots(rows=1, cols=2,
    subplot_titles=["% Gross Profit Margin by Region", "% Net Profit Margin by Region"])

colors_region = px.colors.qualitative.Set2

fig.add_trace(go.Bar(
    x=region["Region"], y=region["Gross_Margin_%"],
    marker_color=colors_region[:5],
    showlegend=False,
    text=region["Gross_Margin_%"].apply(lambda x: f"{x:.1f}%"),
    textposition="outside",
), row=1, col=1)

fig.add_trace(go.Bar(
    x=region["Region"], y=region["Net_Margin_%"],
    marker_color=colors_region[:5],
    showlegend=False,
    text=region["Net_Margin_%"].apply(lambda x: f"{x:.1f}%"),
    textposition="outside",
), row=1, col=2)

fig.update_layout(template="plotly_white", height=400,
    title="Profit Margins by Region, 2015–2024")
fig.show()

Total Revenue and Net Profit by region across all years

Profit Margins by Category (2015–2024)

Code 
cat = df.groupby("Category")[["Total_Revenue","Total_Profit","Net_Profit"]].sum().reset_index()
cat["Gross_%"] = (cat["Total_Profit"] / cat["Total_Revenue"] * 100).round(2)
cat["Net_%"] = (cat["Net_Profit"] / cat["Total_Revenue"] * 100).round(2)
cat = cat.sort_values("Gross_%", ascending=False)

fig = go.Figure()
fig.add_trace(go.Bar(
    name="Gross Profit Margin %",
    x=cat["Category"], y=cat["Gross_%"],
    marker_color="#4C72B0",
))
fig.add_trace(go.Bar(
    name="Net Profit Margin %",
    x=cat["Category"], y=cat["Net_%"],
    marker_color="#DD8452",
))
fig.add_hline(y=0, line_dash="dash", line_color="gray")
fig.update_layout(
    barmode="group",
    title="Gross vs Net Profit Margin by Category, 2015–2024",
    template="plotly_white",
    yaxis_title="Margin (%)",
    height=450,
)
fig.show()

Gross and net profit margins across all 10 product categories

Reflection

This was my first experience working with a real business dataset in a group setting. The most challenging aspect was translating raw financial data into clear, actionable insights for a non-technical audience. I also learned how important it is to look beyond headline metrics — Europe appeared to be the best-performing region in 2024 based on gross margin, but deeper analysis revealed South America’s superior performance when volume was accounted for. Working as a team also taught me the value of dividing analytical tasks logically and integrating findings into a coherent narrative.

Conclusion

This project provided a comprehensive analysis of AgriFood Global’s financial performance across regions and product categories. The findings revealed that profitability is highly concentrated in specific regions, particularly South America, while other regions require strategic improvements.

The analysis also highlighted that revenue growth alone does not guarantee profitability, as factors such as cost management, discounts, and shipping expenses significantly impact net profit.

From a business perspective, focusing on high-margin regions and categories, while improving efficiency in underperforming areas, can lead to more sustainable and profitable growth. This project demonstrates the value of data-driven decision-making in guiding strategic business actions.

import pandas as pd

This project provided a comprehensive analysis of AgriFood Global’s financial performance across regions and product categories. The findings revealed that profitability is highly concentrated in specific regions, particularly South America, while other regions require strategic improvements.

The analysis also highlighted that revenue growth alone does not guarantee profitability, as factors such as cost management, discounts, and shipping expenses significantly impact net profit.

From a business perspective, focusing on high-margin regions and categories, while improving efficiency in underperforming areas, can lead to more sustainable and profitable growth. This project demonstrates the value of data-driven decision-making in guiding strategic business actions.
